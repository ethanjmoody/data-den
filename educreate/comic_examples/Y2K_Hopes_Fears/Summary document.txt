Main lesson objective: Explain some of the most significant fears and hopes that U.S. citizens had on the eve of January 1, 2000.

As Americans approached Y2K, key concerns included:

1. Y2K computer bug: Fear of widespread technological failures
2. Economic instability: Worries about market crashes and financial chaos
3. Infrastructure collapse: Concerns about power grids, transportation, and communication systems failing

Hopeful expectations included:

1. Technological advancements: Excitement for new innovations in the digital age
2. Global unity: Aspirations for increased international cooperation
3. Fresh start: Optimism for solving social and environmental issues in the new millennium

Overall, Y2K represented both anxiety and hope for Americans entering a new era.
